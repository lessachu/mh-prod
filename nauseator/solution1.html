This is an array of 12 coats of arms of Swiss cantons. There are exactly 26 Swiss cantons. They have a definitive constitutional order.

Some sources are a little squidgy on the order because, a few times, two cantons were formed by splitting one canton in half, so they entered simultaneously. But there is an order. The full Wikipedia page “Data Codes for Switzerland” has the proper order, though there seems to be a mobile bug that switches a couple of lines in the chart. AHA – This is a color nonagrams/paint-by-number

Nudge/Sledge – This isn’t meant to be tricky. This follows all the standard rules for this puzzle type.


The numbers ahead of a row or column indicate how many groups of colored squares are contained in each row or column, and how many uninterrupted colored squares each group contains. For example, “4 3 2” means there are, in order, a group of 4 uninterrupted black squares, then a group of 3 uninterrupted black squares, then a group of 2 uninterrupted red squares. Two consecutive groups of the same color must be separated by at least one white square. Two consecutive groups of different colors don’t need to have any white squares between them (but, of course, can have them).
